Introduction to Tea

Tea is an aromatic beverage prepared by pouring hot or boiling water over cured or fresh leaves of Camellia sinensis, an evergreen shrub native to East Asia. Tea is the most widely consumed drink in the world after water. The flavors of tea vary greatly, offering something for every palate.

How to Prepare the Perfect Cup of Tea

Preparing tea can be an art form. Here are some tips to help you brew the perfect cup:

  • Water Temperature: The water temperature is critical. Green teas require cooler water, while black teas need boiling water.
  • Steeping Time: Over-steeping can make your tea bitter. Follow the guidelines for each type of tea.
  • Quality of Tea: Always use high-quality tea leaves for the best flavor.
  • Tea to Water Ratio: Generally, use 1 teaspoon of tea leaves per cup of water.

Types of Tea

There are many varieties of tea, each with unique flavors and benefits:

  1. Green Tea: Light and fresh, rich in antioxidants.
  2. Black Tea: Bold and robust, often enjoyed with milk.
  3. Oolong Tea: A balance between green and black, with a complex flavor.
  4. White Tea: Delicate and subtly sweet, minimally processed.
  5. Herbal Tea: Caffeine-free, made from a blend of herbs, fruits, and spices.
